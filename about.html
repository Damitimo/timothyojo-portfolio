About Me

Background

I earned my Bachelor of Pharmacy with distinction from Obafemi Awolowo University before transitioning into tech through self-teaching. This unique path equipped me with both the empathy to understand people and the structured thinking to build solutions that improve their experiences.

Outside of work

You'll find me exploring LA's food scene, writing reflective essays, or playing chess. I believe diverse experiences inform better product decisions.

My Journey

My journey from healthcare to technology has been driven by a desire to create meaningful impact at scale. As a pharmacist, I learned to understand complex systems and the importance of precision in decision-making. This foundation has been invaluable in product management, where I apply the same analytical rigor to understand user needs and build solutions that truly matter.

At RBC, I had the privilege of leading digital transformation initiatives that directly impacted millions of customers. The experience taught me that great products aren't just about features—they're about understanding the human experience and designing solutions that feel intuitive and empowering.

Now, as I pursue my MBA at USC Marshall, I'm exploring the intersection of health tech, product strategy, and innovation. I'm particularly interested in how emerging technologies can be applied to solve complex healthcare challenges and improve patient outcomes.

My PM Philosophy

I believe in building products that truly resonate with users. My approach combines analytical thinking from my pharmacy background with design sensibility and technical understanding to create solutions that work. Great products emerge from deep user empathy, rigorous data analysis, and cross-functional collaboration.

I prioritize solving real problems over building features, measuring impact over output, and enabling teams over managing tasks. Product management is about being the voice of the user while balancing business objectives and technical constraints—finding elegant solutions in complex systems.
