Creditor Insurance Widget

Designed and implemented a creditor insurance widget to give the insurance department autonomy within banking flows, solving inter-departmental collaboration challenges while optimizing for revenue through strategic in-flow integration.

Client

RBC Insurance & Banking

Date completed

November 2024

Role

Product Manager, RBC

The Challenge Department Dynamics Current State Problems Widget Solution Integration Strategy Placement Decisioning Impact

The challenge

The creditor insurance integration faced a fundamental organizational challenge: two departments with different priorities and ownership models. The banking department controlled the entire customer experience for line of credit and mortgage applications, while the insurance department needed to make changes to insurance-related steps but had to wait for banking team prioritization, creating bottlenecks and delays.

Department dynamics

Understanding the different priorities and constraints of each department was crucial for designing an effective solution.

Banking Department
Owns the complete customer buying experience for financial products (line of credit, mortgage, etc.). Focuses on conversion rates, user experience, and technical integration complexity.
Insurance Department
Needs to make changes to insurance-related steps within banking flows. Requires autonomy to iterate and improve insurance offerings without depending on banking team prioritization.

Current state problems

The existing model created several operational inefficiencies that impacted both departments and ultimately the customer experience.

Dependency Bottlenecks

Insurance team had to wait for banking team to prioritize their requests, leading to delayed improvements and feature releases.

Ownership Conflicts

Banking department owned the entire experience, making it difficult for insurance to iterate on their specific components without affecting the broader flow.

Development Complexity

Any changes to insurance steps required coordination with banking development teams, increasing complexity and reducing agility.

Widget solution

The solution was to create a self-contained creditor insurance widget that could be owned and managed by the insurance department while being seamlessly integrated into banking flows.

Widget Benefits
Consistency: Maintains left navigation bar consistency across all pages, ensuring familiar interface for advisors.

Accessibility: Enables quick switching between sections without returning to dashboard.

Context Preservation: Allows access to other sections while maintaining workflow context.

Responsive Design: Ensures navigation works across devices with collapsible sidebar for smaller screens.

The widget approach minimized development work for the banking team while giving insurance the autonomy they needed to iterate and improve their offerings.

Integration strategy

The widget was designed to integrate seamlessly into existing banking flows with minimal disruption to the banking team's development process.

Minimal Banking Development

The widget required minimal development work from the banking team, focusing on simple integration points rather than complex custom implementations.

Shared Screen Components

Created shared screens for document review and final completion, allowing both departments to maintain their respective experiences within a unified flow.

Independent Iteration

Insurance team could now make changes to their widget without requiring banking team coordination or approval.

Placement decisioning

A critical decision was determining where to insert the widget within the banking flows to optimize for both revenue and user experience.

In-Flow vs. After-Flow Analysis

Evaluated whether to integrate the widget during the line of credit activation or mortgage application process (in-flow) versus after the main banking flow completion (after-flow).

Revenue Optimization

In-flow integration provided higher revenue potential as customers were already engaged in the purchasing process, making them more likely to add insurance products.

Technical Trade-offs

While in-flow required more development work to create shared screens for document review and final completion, the revenue benefits justified the additional technical complexity.

Shared Experience Design

Designed shared screens where document reviews and final confirmations could be handled collaboratively, ensuring both banking and insurance experiences felt integrated rather than separate.

Impact

The creditor insurance widget successfully addressed the inter-departmental collaboration challenges while optimizing for revenue and user experience.

Departmental Autonomy

Insurance department gained the ability to iterate and improve their offerings without waiting for banking team prioritization, significantly reducing time-to-market for insurance improvements.

Revenue Optimization

In-flow integration strategy maximized revenue potential by capturing customers during their peak engagement in the banking flow.

Reduced Development Friction

Minimized development work required from banking teams while creating a sustainable model for future insurance integrations.

Improved User Experience

Shared screens and integrated flows created a seamless experience that felt cohesive rather than fragmented between departments.
