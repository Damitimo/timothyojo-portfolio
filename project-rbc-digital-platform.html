The challenge

RBC Insurance offered three distinct products—Critical Illness, Life, and Disability insurance—each with unique eligibility criteria, workflows, and application processes. Advisors struggled with fragmented systems where each product required separate applications, different eligibility rules, and disconnected workflows. The complexity of managing product dependencies, citizenship requirements, age limits, and health conditions across multiple platforms created inefficiencies and advisor frustration.

Additionally, there was no unified way to handle common elements across products or dynamically adjust available options based on client information and product selections. This led to errors, compliance issues, and a cumbersome experience that drove advisors to choose competitors with more streamlined solutions.

Research

We conducted comprehensive user research with both advisors and clients to understand the complete e-application experience. Our research revealed that the process suffered from high dropout rates due to complexity, lack of personalization, and inadequate error handling. We identified specific pain points including advisor frustration with manual data entry, inconsistent platforms across products, and client discomfort with sharing sensitive information.

Through detailed analysis, we discovered that advisors were spending excessive time on data entry, often entering information twice for joint policies, and struggling with format inconsistencies across different e-application platforms. Field Sales Advisors were particularly impacted, having to relearn different formats for each product and navigate between four separate platforms with varying validation processes.

User Research Insights & Journey Maps

Research methodology

Our research approach focused on understanding both user behavior and system performance through comprehensive data analysis. We established key research questions around drop-off analysis, time analysis, error patterns, user interactions, and A/B testing results. This methodology enabled us to identify specific pain points and measure the effectiveness of our solution iterations.

Key research areas included: analyzing where users most frequently abandon the application process, measuring time spent on each section, identifying common error types and their impact on completion rates, understanding user interaction patterns with help options, and evaluating input completeness across different information types. We also examined return patterns to understand user re-engagement behavior after initial abandonment.

This data-driven approach allowed us to establish an observability practice for ongoing e-application optimization, ensuring our solution addressed real user needs rather than assumptions.

Research Framework & Data Analysis Approach

Key findings

Our research identified six core problems: lack of personalization, complex and lengthy forms, inadequate error handling, insufficient user motivation, client discomfort with sensitive information disclosure, and poor UI/UX across inconsistent platforms. These issues resulted in high dropout rates and advisor frustration, with Field Sales Advisors particularly impacted by having to navigate four different e-application platforms.

Current state analysis

The current state revealed a fragmented ecosystem where Field Sales Advisors had to navigate four different e-application platforms, each with unique formats, validation processes, and user interfaces. Advisors couldn't copy-paste contact information from Salesforce due to format requirements, forcing manual re-entry. Joint policies required duplicate data entry, and inconsistent platform setups meant advisors had to relearn processes for each product.

The digital platform offered API integration and auto-population, while the eDocLite platform required manual processes like obtaining issuer IDs from Moneris for credit card validation. This inconsistency created a two-tiered experience where some products offered streamlined workflows while others remained manual and time-consuming.

Opportunity

The opportunity was to create a universal e-application solution that addressed all six identified problem areas: personalization through dynamic content, simplified and intuitive forms, robust error handling with clear feedback, built-in user motivation through progress indicators, sensitive information handling with privacy-first design, and consistent UI/UX across all products. This would transform the fragmented, frustrating experience into a seamless, advisor-friendly platform.

A universal e-application solution where advisors can seamlessly handle Critical Illness, Life, and Disability insurance products through a single, intelligent platform that dynamically adjusts based on client information and product selections, powered by a sophisticated rules engine and modular architecture.

Solution overview

The universal e-application solution consolidates Critical Illness, Life, and Disability insurance products into a single, dynamic platform. Built on a modular architecture in Salesforce, the system uses a sophisticated rules engine to handle product eligibility, dependencies, and workflows in real-time. The platform intelligently adjusts available options based on client information, citizenship status, age limits, and product selections while maintaining compliance across all regulatory requirements.

Platform Architecture & User Journey

Product architecture

The solution required comprehensive business and product analysis to map eligibility criteria across all three insurance products. We created a detailed matrix documenting age limits, citizenship requirements, health conditions, and occupation restrictions for each product. This analysis revealed common elements like client demographics and shared processes, while identifying unique aspects that required specialized handling.

The technical architecture centers on a dynamic rules engine built in Salesforce that handles product eligibility and dependencies in real-time. Each product (CI, Life, Disability) was configured as a modular component with its own eligibility criteria, workflows, and data fields. The system dynamically combines these modules based on client selections, using conditional logic and dynamic field visibility to guide advisors through the application process.

Key architectural decisions included building flexible configurations to accommodate future products, ensuring regulatory compliance across all workflows, and creating a scalable foundation that could adapt to changing business requirements without requiring system overhauls.

Modular Product Architecture & Rules Engine

Advisor workflow

The platform orchestrates a complex workflow that begins with sales calls and customer lookup in Salesforce. The system intelligently determines whether client information needs updating, pre-fills forms when data exists, and seamlessly integrates with illustration software for policy calculations. When advisors click "Apply" from the illustration tool, they're automatically returned to Salesforce with all relevant data populated, creating a continuous flow from prospect identification through final e-application submission.

Key workflow elements include: client lookup and persona-based searches, automatic information verification and updates, seamless data transfer between Salesforce and illustration software, and intelligent form pre-filling based on existing client data. This eliminates the manual data transfer that previously caused errors and delays.

Key features

The platform features dynamic product selection based on real-time eligibility checking, intelligent form pre-filling using existing client data, and conditional workflows that adapt to product combinations. Key features include a sophisticated rules engine that handles complex product dependencies, modular product configurations that enable easy updates, and comprehensive compliance validation across all regulatory requirements.

The system dynamically adjusts the user interface based on product selections and client information, hiding irrelevant options and surfacing relevant choices. Cross-functional collaboration tools enable business, design, and development teams to maintain and update product rules without requiring system rebuilds.
